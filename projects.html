Centipede (Gameplay Programmer)

(2017) Trying to make a near perfect replica

This was an attempt to make a replica of 1982 Atari Centipede. The point of working on Centipede was to show the difficulty of programming without exact knowledge of Design Patterns. This was the first steps in using Singletons, Flyweight and Command patterns in C++. At the end we needed to have a near identical game given to us for a resource without any errors.

Tonal Shift (UI/FX Programmer)

(2016) Using voice to the world around a ball. Made in Unity

Tonal Shift was the winner of the 2016 Game Jam during the winter in DePaul. It uses Unity and C# to control the players voice to move the floor for a ball to move. The pitch used will move rectangles upward to change the movement of the ball to avoid obstacles. I worked specifically on the level design, environment, and the scripts for color changing and the amount of volume needed to control the ractangles.

MellowMallows (Gameplay Programmer)

(2016) Zen at its finest. Made in Unity

This is a game based on relaxing. There are no win lose scenarios. You toast marshmellows to your will. Brown, Gold or too a crisp. The choice is yours. This is my first game I created in Unity. I was rotating quaternions for arm movement and using a simplified lerp to make a more optimized color change.
