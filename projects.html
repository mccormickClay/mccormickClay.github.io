Ramen

3D Game Engine

Ramen is a basic game engine worked on from a backend that controlled the graphics card in OpenGL. Within the engine I made I worked on a objective tank game to destroy enemies and collect objects or power ups. The Engine has a Terrain manager and creator along with the ability to have sprite, alarms, collision and a keylogger replying and debugging. Some blogs might bring up Ramen as an example.

Tonal Shift

Trying to make a near perfect replica

Tonal Shift was the winner of the 2016 Game Jam during the winter in DePaul. It uses Unity and C# to control the players voice to move the floor for a ball to move. The pitch used will move rectangles upward to change the movement of the ball to avoid obstacles. I worked specifically on the level design, environment, and the scripts for color changing and the amount of volume needed to control the ractangles.
